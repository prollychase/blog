## It Started at Breakfast

dammmn it's funny how some of the worst days begin in the most ordinary places, a restaurant.

Just another college breakfast. Friends around. Noise everywhere. Plates clanking. Fake laughter. Real overthinking.

Everyone around me seemed to be moving ahead in life.

Placements? They had offers.
Careers? Sorted.
Confidence? Overflowing.

And me?

Still refreshing job portals and pretending I was chill.

And yet, sometimes the loudest moments are where silence begins inside you.

Something small happened. Maybe small enough for everyone else to forget by evening. But some moments land differently when your mind is already carrying too much weight .

A few looks changed. A few messages felt sharper than they were meant to be.

What hurt wasn’t just that moment.

It was realizing how easily people can become strangers in a room full of familiarity.

> “Sometimes it’s not one big thing that breaks you. It’s one small thing on the wrong day.”

So I did what any emotionally stable adult would do **ig** 😅.

I came home… and left for Pondicherry by evening.

Anddd, guess what ? My parents had no idea what i was gonna do 🙃.

---

## Main Character Energy (or Poor Decision Making Probably)

My cousin was going, so I joined.

No bag.
No extra clothes.
No charger.
Only shorts, wallet-less confidence, and unresolved feelings.

By the time we reached Pondicherry, I checked my phone.

Messages.

And for reasons still unknown to science, I walked to the beach and threw my phone into the sea.

Yes.

Into the sea. (fml)

Like as if I was the main character ending a movie scene no one asked for.

Then two seconds later:

Realization hits..

"Wait... my phone. 😭😭"

So I somehow retrieved it.

Wet. Broken. Spiritually exhausted.

---

## Me vs my luv( ahem obviously my phone )

Next morning, I submitted it to a service center.

Meanwhile:

- Missed calls from family
- Missed calls from friends

Got it fixed(Thats what they said 🙃).

Then realized:

Camera dead.
Cell service dead.
Trust in life? Also dead....

Submitted it again.

My cousin had to leave for Chennai.

And there I was.

Alone in Pondicherry.
No working phone.
No proper clothes.
No emotional roadmap.

And... just vibes.

---

## Walking Through a City While Lost in Another One

First i bought myself a new shorts and a hawaiian shirt (Billy Butcher vibes locked in 🤣) using my cousin's card like a financially sponsored philosopher.

I roamed the streets.

Explored the white town.

Walked the beach roads. Sat near the sea. Tried a few cafes.

And god all I missed was a working camera 🥲.

And somewhere between salty air and silence, things felt lighter.

There's something healing about being in a place where nobody knows your name, your career or what happened at the lunch.

> "Sometimes you don't need answers. You need distance."

The sunset there reminded me of idk what lol .

Beautiful, warm, impossible to hold, and gone before I understood it.

Dramatic? Yes.
Accurate? Also yes.
Emotional ? Maybe a little 😅.

---

## The Night Everything Calmed Down

By night, I finally got my phone back.

Spoke to a close friend.

One of those conversations that don't solve your problems, but rearrange your thoughts.

They were worried, obviously.

Which is when I remembered:

No matter how lost you feel, there are always people counting the hours until you return.

---

## What I Learned

I came back home the next morning.

Same problems.

But different mind.

Learnings??

### 1. Comparison is silent poison

Watching everyone get their careers sorted made me feel late. But life doesn't run on my timeline. (And thats ok).

### 2. Some silences say more than words ever could

Sometimes people don’t realize what they changed in someone, simply because they walked away unchanged themselves.

### 3. Getting away helps... briefly

A different city can calm your mind. But eventually, you still meet yourself there.

### 4. Never throw your phone into the sea

This one is practical.

---

## Final Thought

That trip looked chaotic from outside.

And it was.

But you know what? Probab

Sometimes the road trip isn't about escape.

It's about returning with less weight than you left with.

> "I went to Pondicherry to forget a day.
> I came back remembering who I was."

And yes... I still cringe about the phone part.
